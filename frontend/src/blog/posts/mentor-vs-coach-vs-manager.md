Many professionals confuse mentors, coaches, and managers. While all three support growth, their roles are very different.

Understanding these differences helps you choose the right kind of guidance for your career.

## What Is a Manager?

A manager:

- Oversees your work
- Assigns tasks
- Evaluates performance
- Focuses on organisational goals

Managers are responsible for results, not personal career direction.

## What Is a Coach?

A coach:

- Works on specific skills
- Focuses on short-term improvement
- Often follows a structured program
- Is usually paid for outcomes

Coaching is ideal when you want to improve one specific skill.

## What Is a Mentor?

A mentor:

- Shares experience and perspective
- Guides long-term career growth
- Acts as a sounding board
- Supports decision-making

Mentorship is relationship-based, not task-based.
